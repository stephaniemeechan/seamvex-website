## Email body (Stephanie)

Dear [Name / team],

I wanted to write to you personally.

You may already know that I founded **Seamvex Data Systems Ltd** to run Seamcor properly — with the same software many of you have relied on for years, but with a steadier, more personal way of doing business. That is what you see on [seamcor.com](https://seamcor.com) today: **Seamvex Data Systems Ltd, trading as Seamcor**.

### What this means for you

- **Same Seamcor software** — no change to your platform or how you use it day to day.
- **Same people looking after you** — support, account management, and implementation continue as before.
- **Same contact details** — support@seamcor.com, accounts@seamcor.com, and our usual phone number (+44 7392 991808).

### What is changing behind the scenes

Seamvex now holds the licence to distribute and support Seamcor software. All customer-facing work — support, billing, renewals, and account management — is handled by **Seamvex Data Systems Ltd, trading as Seamcor**.

Some of your existing paperwork may still show **Seamcor Limited** as the contracting company. That is an administrative hangover from the old structure, not a change to what you receive from us. Over the coming [weeks / months], we will send a **short, simple update** so your agreement reflects **Seamvex Data Systems Ltd** as your supplier. **Your terms, pricing, and service levels stay the same** — this is housekeeping, not a renegotiation.

If you have any questions, reply to this email or contact us on the details above. I am happy to speak to anyone who would like a direct conversation.

Thank you for your continued trust in Seamcor.

Kind regards,

**Stephanie Meechan**  
Founder & Director  
Seamvex Data Systems Ltd, trading as Seamcor  
+44 7392 991808 · support@seamcor.com

## What not to say

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| “Your contract has been transferred to Seamvex” | Requires customer signature on updated agreement |
| “Nothing changes for you legally” | Supplier entity is changing until they sign |
| “Seamvex owns the Seamcor trademark” | IP is with Rooikat; Seamvex holds exclusive licence to distribute and use the mark |
| Leading with Rooikat | Customers deal with Seamvex / Seamcor; only mention Rooikat if asked about IP ownership |
